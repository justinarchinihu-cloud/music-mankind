Album Bio

This record came about when my friend and producer, D’Andre Neal, messaged me about recording. He asked if me and my band wanted to come to Valencia College, where he works as a part of the studio’s staff. We set a date and I began to write some music. Originally this was supposed to be a quartet record with my band that was developed in Jacksonville Florida. The group consisted of Tyler Snell on Tenor, Justin Archinihu on bass, Ethan Grussgott on drums, and myself. The tunes I wrote for this session had that band in mind.

Tyler wasn't able to make the session, so I decided we should just do it all trio. The tunes I wrote worked surprisingly well for trio playing. They lent themselves to interaction. Trio playing, to me, is all about interacting. With such a small ensemble the music doesn't need to be solo after solo. Rather, the trio setting lends itself to the songs unfolding on their own. Soloing becomes less important than the ensemble working together as a whole. I imagine trio playing like three people rowing a boat. We may be using the paddles all differently but the overall projection of the boat is the focus.

I wrote all the tunes on this record besides one. Ethan Grussgott wrote the tune “Ronin”. To me this tune is how I wanted the whole record to feel. Ethan didn't like it. I think sometimes it takes someone else bringing something to the table to take you out of yourself and see what you were really going for. I tried to do something similar to Ethan’s tune on my tune, “Aomame”. For the first 8 measures of my tune there are no changes. Ethan’s tune has no changes at all — just a melody and time. It's completely free. At that point in my life I was too afraid to write something completely free, even though it was the feeling I was going for. I was scared of letting go of my idea of music, or at least what I thought my music was. These days I am learning how to let go of all my ideas of music. To be free.

This record was also a sort of a farewell to friends and an era of life. Ethan, Justin, and I had spent the past three years of our lives learning how to play music together and we were all going our separate ways in the months coming after this recording. Ethan moved to Colorado, Justin stayed in Jacksonville, and I moved to New York. Those two guys were the two people I spent most of my time with for those years. We had moments of being pissed at each other, loving each other, trying to understand each other, not understanding each other, and finally trying to accept each other. When I listen back to this record I can hear all of that in it.

I've struggled with whether I should release this recording for a while now. I am not super happy with my playing here and I feel like I have gotten much better since then. But I can hear us fighting with each other and ourselves here. I can hear each of our personalities in this recording. We were really trying to find ourselves and how we fit in with one another here. This record may not be where I wanted it to be on a technical level but I think this recording is interesting and tells a story of friendship and angst. We were and are still young, stubborn, and always learning.
